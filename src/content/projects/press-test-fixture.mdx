## Overview

A critical sub-assembly in an electromechanical device required press-fitting a stainless steel pin into an injection-molded housing. Manual inspection was subjective and catching only 60% of mis-presses before final assembly.

## Problem

The existing process used a manual go/no-go check after press-fit. Defect escapes reached 4.2% at end-of-line and 1.1% at customer site — a regulatory and reliability risk for a Class II device.

## Approach

Designed a poka-yoke press fixture with:
- Precision-ground tooling plate with ±0.001" feature location
- Load cell integrated into the press head for force-displacement curve capture
- Real-time pass/fail display tied to a parametric force window (SPC-derived)
- GD&T tolerance stack analysis to confirm fixture clearances and pin engagement length

DFM review with the contract manufacturer confirmed the fixture was buildable within a 4-week lead time at under $6K tooling cost.

## Outcome

- Defect escape rate: 4.2% → 0.3%
- Inspection cycle time: −55% (from 22s to 10s per unit)
- Zero field returns attributed to press-fit defects in 18 months post-launch
- Fixture design adopted across two additional sub-assemblies
