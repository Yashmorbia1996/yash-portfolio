---
title: "Thermal Redesign Journey"
summary: |
  A deployed product was overheating in the field, driving customer returns to 23%. I led a two-phase mechanical redesign to fix the thermal path instead of applying workarounds.

  Phase 1 focused on airflow: grill geometry was iterated in SolidWorks Flow Simulation, then validated physically.

  Phase 2 addressed the internal stack (semiconductor package and heatsink path) to remove the junction-to-case bottleneck.

  Together, the work cut chip temperature by 16°C and reduced return rate from 23% to below 3%. Both phases shipped to production.

  <div class="flex flex-col gap-6">
    <div class="flex flex-col overflow-hidden rounded-2xl border border-border bg-background">
      <div class="flex flex-1 flex-col px-5 py-6 md:px-6 md:py-7">

        <p class="mt-4 text-sm leading-relaxed text-text-secondary md:text-[15px] md:leading-relaxed">
          Identified more thermally efficient semiconductor chip in <strong class="text-text-primary">TO-9</strong> package through supplier evaluation. New chip offered lower junction-to-case thermal resistance (<strong class="text-text-primary">Rθjc</strong>), directly reducing heat generated at source under equivalent operating conditions.
        </p>
      </div>
    </div>

        <p class="mt-2 text-sm leading-relaxed text-text-secondary">
          Replaced the plastic-plus-metal heatsink with an all-metal conduction path, removing the thermal bottleneck between the chip and the enclosure.
