{/* ── THE PROBLEM ── */}

<div id="the-problem" class="not-prose mb-16 scroll-mt-28">
  <h2 class="text-4xl font-semibold tracking-[-0.04em] text-text-primary md:text-5xl lg:text-6xl">
    The problem
  </h2>
  <h3 class="mt-4 text-2xl font-semibold tracking-[-0.03em] text-text-primary md:text-3xl">
    Six failure modes. One root cause.
  </h3>
  <p class="mt-4 max-w-3xl text-base leading-relaxed text-text-secondary">
    Badge reels fail in predictable ways. I mapped six failure modes and ranked them by severity before touching any CAD. The most critical: <strong class="text-text-primary">snap-back</strong>. When a clock spring releases, the cord returns at 4 to 5 m/s. That is the cord acting as a hammer, and it is responsible for almost every downstream failure mode: fraying, fatigue, jamming, and user dissatisfaction.
  </p>

  <div class="mt-6 grid gap-4 sm:grid-cols-3">
    <div class="rounded-2xl border border-border bg-surface-elevated/50 p-5">

      <p class="mt-2 font-semibold text-text-primary">Snap-Back</p>

    </div>
    <div class="rounded-2xl border border-border bg-surface-elevated/50 p-5">

{/* ── KEY DESIGN DECISION ── */}

<div id="spring-selection" class="not-prose mb-16 scroll-mt-28">
  <div class="mb-2 flex flex-wrap items-center gap-3">
    <span class="inline-flex rounded-md bg-primary-accent/10 px-2 py-1 font-mono text-[10px] font-semibold uppercase tracking-[0.14em] text-primary-accent">
      Key decision
    </span>
    <h2 class="text-3xl font-semibold tracking-[-0.03em] text-text-primary md:text-4xl">
      Clock spring vs. constant force spring
    </h2>
    <span class="h-px min-w-[2rem] flex-1 bg-border" />
  </div>

  <p class="mt-4 max-w-3xl text-base leading-relaxed text-text-secondary">
    The clock spring is the root cause of snap-back. At full extension it stores maximum elastic energy and releases it as a sharp 2 to 3 times force spike. A <strong class="text-text-primary">constant force (CF) spring</strong> eliminates that spike: it delivers the same retraction force at any extension length. Switching spring type alone drops snap speed by 19%.
  </p>
  <p class="mt-4 max-w-3xl text-base leading-relaxed text-text-secondary">
    This was the single most important design decision. Every concept that followed was built on a CF spring foundation.
  </p>

  <div class="mt-6 grid grid-cols-1 gap-4 sm:grid-cols-2">
    <div class="overflow-hidden rounded-2xl border border-border bg-white p-4">
      <img src="/images/badge-reel-photo-light-open.png" alt="Badge reel disassembled showing clock spring inside" class="mx-auto block h-auto w-full max-h-56 rounded-xl object-contain" loading="lazy" />
      <p class="mt-2 text-center text-xs text-text-muted">Clock spring inside a disassembled badge reel. Stores peak energy at full extension.</p>
    </div>
    <div class="overflow-hidden rounded-2xl border border-border bg-white p-4">
      <img src="/images/badge-reel-spring-cf-photo.png" alt="Constant force spring" class="mx-auto block h-auto w-full max-h-56 rounded-xl object-contain" loading="lazy" />
      <p class="mt-2 text-center text-xs text-text-muted">Constant force spring. Flat force profile across full stroke.</p>
    </div>
  </div>

  <div class="mt-4 mx-auto max-w-sm overflow-hidden rounded-2xl border border-border bg-white p-4">
    <img src="/images/badge-reel-spring-graph.png" alt="Force-deflection comparison: clock spring vs constant force spring" class="mx-auto block h-auto w-full rounded-xl object-contain" loading="lazy" />
    <p class="mt-2 text-center text-xs text-text-muted">Force-deflection profiles compared. CF spring eliminates the peak force spike.</p>
  </div>

  <div class="mt-6 flex flex-col overflow-hidden rounded-2xl border border-border bg-surface-elevated/50">
    <div class="border-l-4 border-primary-accent px-5 py-5 md:px-6 md:py-6">
      <p class="font-mono text-[10px] font-semibold uppercase tracking-[0.16em] text-primary-accent">Spring specification</p>
      <p class="mt-3 text-base leading-relaxed text-text-secondary md:text-[17px]">
        Fully calculated: 301 stainless steel, t = 0.08mm, w = 5.58mm, designed for 120g load at 600mm cord length. FOS = 5.13. Stress ratio = 19% of yield. Rated for 50K+ cycles and 5 to 6 year service life. All constraints verified.
      </p>
    </div>
  </div>
</div>

<div id="recommended" class="not-prose mb-16 scroll-mt-28">
  <div class="mb-2 flex flex-wrap items-center gap-3">
    <span class="inline-flex rounded-md bg-primary-accent/10 px-2 py-1 font-mono text-[10px] font-semibold uppercase tracking-[0.14em] text-primary-accent">
      Recommended
    </span>
    <h2 class="text-3xl font-semibold tracking-[-0.03em] text-text-primary md:text-4xl">
      D3: Three independent damping layers
    </h2>
    <span class="h-px min-w-[2rem] flex-1 bg-border" />
  </div>

  <p class="mt-4 max-w-3xl text-base leading-relaxed text-text-secondary">
    D3 stacks three mechanisms that each work independently, so if one is slightly off-spec, the others still absorb the energy. The CF spring controls the retraction profile throughout the stroke. The TPU gasket adds consistent friction across the full pull length. The bumper spring at the last 8 to 10mm catches whatever energy remains and brings the cord to a complete stop.
  </p>
