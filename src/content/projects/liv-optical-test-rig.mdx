<div id="the-problem" class="not-prose mb-12 scroll-mt-28">
  <h2 class="text-4xl font-semibold tracking-[-0.04em] text-text-primary md:text-5xl lg:text-6xl">
    The problem
  </h2>
  <h3 class="mt-4 text-2xl font-semibold tracking-[-0.03em] text-text-primary md:text-3xl">
    No fast, trustworthy batch screen before committing engines to the line
  </h3>
  <p class="mt-4 max-w-3xl text-base leading-relaxed text-text-secondary">
    The laser engine needed repeatable LIV screening at volume, without measurement noise that looked like bad parts. We also needed a practical way to check <strong class="text-text-primary">beam shape and size</strong>, not only total power, before release.
  </p>
  <p class="mt-4 max-w-3xl text-base leading-relaxed text-text-secondary">
    The legacy flow was manual and operator-sensitive. The goal was AQL-friendly stations: quick load/unload, stable coupling, and clear handoff to software for technician-ready runs.
  </p>
</div>
