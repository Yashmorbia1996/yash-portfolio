## Overview

This project delivered the **LIV (Light–Current–Voltage) optical test rig** used to characterize illuminator performance in a regulated product context. Work progressed from a bench-level feasibility setup through a first structured rig to a **production-optimized batch test fixture**, with measurement architecture and datums defined early so tolerance and alignment strategy stayed controlled through release.

## Problem

LIV optical measurement had to be proven **before** committing to full mechanical design. An ad hoc bench setup could show the principle but not **repeatable alignment**, and the first structured rig exposed **thermal and vibration sensitivities** that would have undermined production data if left unaddressed. The program needed a **production configuration** with clear GD&T, a traceable BOM, and verification (motion, interference, exploded review) suitable for manufacturing handoff.

## Approach

The evolution followed three deliberate stages:

**1. Working concept** — Bench-level proof of concept using **off-the-shelf hardware** to validate the LIV optical measurement principle, confirm feasibility, and pin down **core alignment requirements** before formal CAD. *Decision:* Lock **measurement architecture** and **critical alignment datums** first—no CAD until that baseline existed.

**2. First design (optical rig)** — Translated the concept into a **structured optical test rig** on a **precision mount base with adjustable stages**. Repeatability trials informed the next iteration, including **thermal and vibration** effects. *Decision:* **Kinematic mounting** with **repeatable datums** to cut **operator-induced alignment variability**.

**3. Optimized batch test design** — Full **SolidWorks** assembly for a **production-oriented fixture** aimed at faster **load / unload / connection** and **shorter time per test cycle**. *Decision:* Fixture architecture targeted a **3× throughput increase** for batch testing.

**Production release (Configuration 3)** — **LIV Tester — final configuration**: **production-released GD&T drawing**, full assembly with a **39-item BOM** spanning **optical, mechanical, and electrical** subassemblies. Completed **motion study**, **interference check**, and **exploded view** review on the final build.

## Outcome

- **3× throughput** improvement for batch LIV testing versus the prior approach, driven by fixture architecture and cycle-time focus  
- **Kinematic, datum-driven alignment** replacing operator-dependent setup for stable optical coupling  
- **Production package**: Config 3 LIV tester, **39-part BOM**, and documented assembly checks ready for manufacturing  
- **ISO 13485–aligned** quality context, with **GD&T** and **tolerance stack-up** supporting release documentation
